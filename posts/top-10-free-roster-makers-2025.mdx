For small business owners, managing staff shifts is often a time-consuming headache. In 2025, the demand for **visual scheduling** and **no-signup tools** has skyrocketed. You need a solution that is fast, reliable, and—most importantly—cost-effective.

Here is our curated list of the top 10 free roster makers that will help you reclaim your time this year.

## 2. Homebase
A popular choice for retail and hospitality, Homebase offers basic scheduling and time tracking for one location. While powerful, its free tier is limited compared to the flexibility of a dedicated builder.

## 3. Sling
Sling provides robust shift scheduling with internal communication features. It is great for teams that need to chat within the app, though the interface can feel a bit crowded for simple tasks.

## 4. 7shifts
Specifically designed for the restaurant industry, 7shifts offers a free tier for up to 30 employees. It excels at managing specific restaurant needs like labor-to-sales ratios.

## 5. Deputy
Deputy is known for its mobile-first approach. The free version allows for basic shift planning but often hides more advanced automation behind a paywall.

## 6. Clockify
Primarily a time tracker, Clockify has expanded into scheduling. It is ideal for freelancers or small agencies that bill by the hour.

## 7. ABC Roster
A classic, no-frills desktop application. It lacks the modern cloud-sync features of TrySchedule but remains a solid offline choice for local-only management.

## 8. Connecteam
An all-in-one employee app. Its scheduling feature is comprehensive but may be "overkill" for managers who just want a fast, printable work schedule.

## 9. Microsoft Excel (Templates)
While not an automated tool, many still use Excel. However, manual entry is prone to errors and lacks the **conflict detection** found in modern builders.

## 10. Google Sheets
Great for collaboration, but like Excel, it lacks a dedicated **visual interface** for dragging and dropping shifts efficiently.
