Acme Corporation, a mid-sized tech company with 200 employees, was drowning in meetings. The average employee spent 23 hours per week in meetingsâ€”leaving precious little time for actual work. Here's how they turned things around.

## The Challenge

Like many growing companies, Acme had developed a meeting-heavy culture. Decision-making required sync-ups, sync-ups required status updates, and status updates became weekly rituals that no one questioned.

**The symptoms were clear:**
- Calendar fragmentation (few blocks longer than 30 minutes)
- Meeting fatigue and declining engagement
- Critical work happening after hours
- Top talent citing "too many meetings" in exit interviews

## The Solution

Acme partnered with us to implement a comprehensive scheduling overhaul. The approach combined tool adoption with cultural change.

### Phase 1: Audit

We helped Acme audit their existing meetings. The findings were eye-opening:
- 60% of recurring meetings had no clear agenda
- 40% of attendees in any given meeting were optional
- Average meeting ran 15 minutes over scheduled time

### Phase 2: Policy Implementation

Based on the audit, Acme implemented new policies:
- "No Meeting Wednesdays" for deep work
- Maximum 25-minute meetings (with hard stops)
- Required agendas for any meeting with 3+ attendees

### Phase 3: Tool Adoption

Schedule Builder became the central hub for all scheduling decisions. Key capabilities deployed:
- Automatic conflict detection
- Meeting cost calculator (showing aggregate salary time)
- Smart suggestions for optimal meeting times

## The Results

After three months, the transformation was measurable:

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Weekly meeting hours | 23 hrs | 14 hrs | -40% |
| Focus time blocks (2+ hrs) | 2/week | 6/week | +200% |
| Meeting satisfaction score | 5.2/10 | 7.8/10 | +50% |

## Lessons Learned

1. **Tools aren't enough:** Cultural buy-in from leadership was essential
2. **Start with quick wins:** No Meeting Wednesdays showed immediate impact
3. **Measure and share:** Sharing metrics weekly kept the momentum
